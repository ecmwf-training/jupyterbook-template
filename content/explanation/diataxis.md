# Diátaxis framework

Writing good documentation is hard. Different users have different needs, and a one-size-fits-all approach often fails to satisfy anyone. When we write documentation, we often have a bias towards our own needs as authors, how we--internally--have structured and understood the subject matter. But not always does that align with the needs of our users. Users may come to our documentation, learning resource or notebooks because they have a specific need. They may want to learn a new concept, complete a specific task, look up a fact, or understand the rationale behind a design choice. Each of these needs requires a different approach to documentation. This is where the Diátaxis framework comes in.
The [Diátaxis framework](https://diataxis.fr/) is a systematic approach to structuring documentation into four distinct types, each serving a specific purpose and addressing different user needs. The four types are:

- [Tutorials](https://diataxis.fr/tutorials/) (learning-oriented): guided walkthroughs for newcomers.
- [How-to guides](https://diataxis.fr/how-to-guides/) (task-oriented): practical steps for common tasks.
- [Reference](https://diataxis.fr/reference/) (information-oriented): standards, conventions, and templates to copy.
- [Explanation](https://diataxis.fr/explanations/) (understanding-oriented): context and rationale behind choices in this template.

Our aim is to adopt the Diátaxis framework more widely across our training and documentation resources, to ensure that we meet the diverse needs of our users effectively. Therefore, we highly recommend that you also structure your Jupyter Book using the Diátaxis framework, to ensure that it meets future requirements.
